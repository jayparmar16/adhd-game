# FLUX / STAX / PULSE — strategy & evidence

Three free, open-source **flow-state games** for adults with ADHD, routed by a 3-question check-in: **FLUX** (gravity-flip runner), **STAX** (falling-block stacker), **PULSE** (tempo-tap ring). Play a few minutes to get *in the zone*, then ride that primed state straight into your real work. A calm focus timer + growing garden catches the handoff.

*Not a medical device. Not a treatment for ADHD. A wellness tool informed by published research.*

> **Three layers, one app.** The **check-in** routes you to the game that matches your current state. The **game** (FLUX/STAX/PULSE) drives arousal, dopamine, and flow. **Alongside** (the focus timer + garden, built first) is the handoff destination where that primed state gets spent on real work. The later sections of this document (body doubling, CBT, anti-burnout design) govern the handoff layer and roadmap; the sections below cover the check-in and the three games.

## Why a check-in — ADHD as a regulation problem, not a fixed deficit

ADHD isn't simply "can't pay attention" — it's **unstable arousal regulation**. **Optimal stimulation theory** frames restlessness and hyperactivity as responses to chronic underarousal ([Zentall & Zentall, 1983](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/21975989_Optimal_stimulation_as_theoretical_basis_of_hyperactivity)), and EEG studies confirm ADHD brains run **unstable, not just low, baseline arousal** ([PMC2785488](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC2785488/)). Critically, this instability plays out **within the same day** — the same person can swing from overstimulated (wired, racing) to understimulated (flat, bored) to underactivated (foggy, stalled) and back ([HealthHero overview](https://www.healthhero.ie/blog/adhd-overstimulation-and-understimulation)). A single fixed game can only ever match one of those states well.

So instead of one primer, there are three, and a 3-tap check-in (always skippable) reads which side of the arousal curve you're on and routes accordingly:

| State | Arousal | Game | Mechanism |
|---|---|---|---|
| Racing / wired / too many tabs | Overstimulated | **STAX** | Visuospatial load, no stakes, absorb the noise |
| Flat / bored / nothing grabs you | Understimulated | **FLUX** | Fast, real stakes, feed the stimulation-seeking |
| Foggy / heavy / can't start | Underactivated | **PULSE** | External rhythm, ramps arousal upward |

Each answer votes for a state; a 1/1/1 tie is broken by the third question specifically, since a stated need ("what should the next few minutes do?") should outweigh two ambiguous earlier reads.

## FLUX — for the bored / understimulated state, the flow mechanism deliberately engineered

Flow is a brain state with known signatures, and every design choice targets one:

- **Transient hypofrontality** — in flow, the self-monitoring prefrontal cortex *downshifts*, quieting the inner critic ([Frontiers 2021, PMC8079660](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC8079660/)). This needs *continuous, reactive motion with no dead time* → the runner never stops, restart is instant (tap to go again), menus are minimal.
- **Locus coeruleus / norepinephrine** — sharpens attention *without* tipping into anxiety. So: **real stakes** (one mistake ends the run) paired with **frictionless retry** (no penalty, no wait) — pressure that focuses, doesn't stress.
- **Dopamine rides on reward anticipation and near-misses** → rising speed, a **near-miss multiplier** (thread a gap by a hair, score jumps and sparks fly), and celebratory peaks.
- **Challenge ≈ skill** — flow lives in the narrow band between boredom and overwhelm. **Dynamic Difficulty Adjustment** keeps you there ([Zohaib 2018, *Advances in HCI*](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1155/2018/5681652)); this is EndeavorOTC's "gets harder as you play," generalized. FLUX persists a skill level between runs (a staircase targeting ~25-second runs) so the difficulty converges on *your* edge.
- **One-button mastery** — the whole game is "tap to flip." Zero learning curve, unlimited skill ceiling (VVVVVV / Gravity Guy lineage). Immediate feedback on every input is exactly what an ADHD reward circuit needs to stay engaged; "ADHD brains self-medicate through the neurochemistry of flow."
- **Game feel is a therapeutic mechanism, not polish** — screen shake, particle trails, hit-stop on crash, glow/bloom, parallax neon horizon. Sensory intensity is what pulls attention past the point of self-consciousness.
- **A single gaming session measurably spikes dopamine and sharpens attention right after** — raised prefrontal activation, ventral-striatum dopamine, improved selective attention post-play ([Frontiers Psychiatry 2025, fNIRS](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychiatry/articles/10.3389/fpsyt.2025.1640142/full)). That post-play window is the point of the handoff.
- **Optional: your own music underneath** — paste a YouTube link and it plays as a backing track (audio only; the game is the visual). A flow multiplier, kept optional so it never blocks play.
- **The honest caveats:** the *transfer* to your task is plausible from the priming/arousal literature, not proven for this specific design; and a dopamine game can itself become the procrastination — so FLUX nudges toward the handoff the longer you play (gentle, never locked), and the reflect step logs a personal primed-vs-unprimed signal. See "Risks & honesty."

## STAX — for the racing / wired state, visuospatial load as a rumination brake

A wired, overstimulated mind is usually a *verbally* racing one — looping thoughts, rehearsed sentences, too many tabs open in the inner monologue. STAX targets that specific channel:

- **Working-memory modality competition.** Rumination and worry run largely on the brain's *verbal* loop (Baddeley's phonological loop); a demanding *visuospatial* task recruits a different channel and directly competes for the same limited working-memory resource, crowding the verbal loop out ([task-modality competition, PMC4550779](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC4550779/)). High visuospatial load has been shown to *eradicate* emotional/threat interference that persists under low load ([PubMed 23750133](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23750133/)).
- **The Tetris lineage specifically.** Tetris-style visuospatial tasks are the best-studied instance of this effect — shown to reduce the frequency/vividness of intrusive memories and cravings when played during the consolidation window ([Tetris: A Next Generation Stressbuster](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/314239176_Tetris_A_Next_Generation_Stressbuster)), and casual block/puzzle games measurably lower physiological stress and depressive symptoms in RCTs (avg. **57% reduction** in depression symptoms in one trial; [Russoniello et al., ECU 2011](https://news.ecu.edu/2011/02/16/study-casual-video-games-demonstrate-ability-to-reduce-depression-and-anxiety/)).
- **Design follows from the mechanism, not from "Tetris is popular."** What makes STAX calming is the **absence of stakes**, not slowness: topping out is a **soft board-clear, not a crash** — no shake, no failure screen, no score reset. Load, by contrast, *should* scale with you, because crowding out the verbal loop is the whole mechanism and a board that's too slow leaves working memory free to ruminate. So the DDA raises the fall rate as you improve, while the no-fail floor keeps it absorbing rather than stressful.
- **The honest caveat:** the rumination-interruption evidence is strongest for the *acute* moment (playing *during* a spike of intrusive thought), not as a lasting trait-level fix — consistent with STAX's framing as a short primer, not a therapy.

## PULSE — for the foggy / can't-start state, rhythm as an activation ramp

Task-initiation paralysis is a *low-arousal* problem: the system needs a nudge upward before it can engage, not more information or willpower. PULSE is built directly on the clinical playbook for this:

- **Rhythmic stimulation raises arousal toward optimal in ADHD** — music's structured, predictable beat is a documented way to nudge an underactivated system upward ([Frontiers Psychology 2025, rhythm/ADHD mechanisms](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2025.1476928/full)).
- **ADHD is linked to specific rhythm-timing deficits**, and rhythm-based training targets that shared mechanism directly ([feasibility protocol, PMC10423996](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10423996/)).
- **This mirrors the standard clinical advice for task paralysis**: give the brain a small, structured dopamine hit before the real task, and start absurdly small — a two-minute high-energy activity, then a five-minute commitment, is the field's own "warm-up" prescription ([Tiimo task-initiation guide](https://www.tiimoapp.com/resource-hub/task-initiation-adhd)). PULSE *is* that warm-up: it starts at a slow, unthreatening 60 bpm (a resting heartbeat) and only ramps to ~132 bpm as hits land — small wins compounding into momentum, exactly the "each microtask delivers a small dopamine boost" mechanism behind the 5-minute rule.
- **Misses widen the window and ease the tempo back**, rather than punishing — a foggy brain that's already struggling to start doesn't need a fail state on top of it.

## Why this exists

- **EndeavorOTC** is the first and only FDA-cleared adult ADHD digital therapeutic (June 2024), delivered as a video game — and it sits behind **$24.99/month or $129.99/year** ([Managed Healthcare Executive 2024](https://www.managedhealthcareexecutive.com/view/first-ever-therapeutic-video-game-for-adult-adhd-approved-by-fda); [Akili/BusinessWire 2024](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20240618578815/en/)). ADHD disproportionately makes recurring subscriptions hard to sustain; a tool to help people follow through shouldn't require flawless follow-through on a bill.
- We looked at what real ADHD adults actually adopt and keep using, and built for that — not for what looks impressive in a study.

## What we learned from the research (and why we're not building another brain-training game)

**Brain-training games are "tested" but don't stick.** Dual n-back has adult ADHD RCTs and the top open-source implementation (Brain Workshop, 345★) — but the paradigm is known for "high difficulty → early dropout." A meta-analysis of 36 RCTs of computerized cognitive training in ADHD found that once outcomes were blinded, working-memory training's effect on inattention collapsed (SMD 0.47 → 0.17) with **no far transfer** ([*Molecular Psychiatry* 2023](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10208955/); [MDPI Brain Sci 2025](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC12468938/)). EndeavorOTC itself had **34% dropout** in the adult trial ([*npj Mental Health Research* 2024](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11187123/)).

**The community proves it.** The curated [awesome-adhd list](https://github.com/XargsUK/awesome-adhd) contains **zero** attention-training games. ADHD adults reach for focus timers, task RPGs, and body doubling instead.

**Body doubling has the strongest signal — and no dominant free/open-source option exists.**
- **Effect:** task efficiency **+27–30%** with a body double in a controlled study; the same gain held for both human and AI-avatar doubles ([arXiv 2509.12153, 2025](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.12153v1)). A 2025 EEG study measured brain activity during body doubling in 26 participants, and CHADD is running its own study.
- **How work *feels*:** "Body doubling influences how work feels beyond objective metrics." Behavioral efficiency improved even when survey measures didn't catch it.
- **Design signals from the paper:**
  - Users split by anxiety — some need low-pressure ambient presence, others thrive on high-accountability social comparison. Both worked; the mismatch is what kills sessions.
  - Visible shared progress > camera feed (~75% ignored the secondary camera).
  - Event-tied captions (idle, progress milestone, mistake) reduced boredom and loneliness without forcing interaction.
  - Rigid scheduling was a named failure mode.
- **Market gap:** [Focusmate](https://focusmate.com) (3 free sessions/week then paid), Flow Club, FLOWN, Caveday, BuckleTime — all commercial SaaS. No dominant free/self-hostable equivalent.

**The strongest adult evidence for symptom improvement is digital CBT/skills** — the *attexis* RCT (N=337) hit **d=0.85** at 3 months (d=0.61 at 6 months) with only **9% dropout** ([*Psychological Medicine* 2025](https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/psychological-medicine/article/effectiveness-of-attexis-a-digital-intervention-based-on-cognitive-behavioral-therapy-for-adults-with-adhd-a-randomized-controlled-trial/BBB55FF99ADF58005B1BD9B00AD0AF96)). Sequence: psychoeducation → attention techniques → self-image. This is a Phase 2 layer.

**Gamification without guardrails hurts ADHD users.** SDT research: rewards, streaks, and punishments tap "introjected regulation" (guilt/shame) and produce burnout and dependence rather than intrinsic motivation ([*BMC* 2024 gamified education anxiety review, PMC12913498](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12913498/); [Yu-kai Chou on SDT](https://yukaichou.com/gamification-analysis/self-determination-theory-guide-to-ryan-and-decis-motivation-framework/)). Habitica reviews document the same trap: "hyperfocus → ADHD burnout, dependence on the app for motivation" ([Choosing Therapy 2024](https://www.choosingtherapy.com/habitica-app-review/)). Our gamification is designed around this evidence, not around it.

## What we're building

Three connected pieces: the **check-in** (routes to the right state of mind), the **games** (FLUX/STAX/PULSE — spike dopamine, induce flow, hand off), and **Alongside** — the calm focus companion they hand off *to*.

### The games, collectively

- **Flow needs challenge ≈ skill, and dynamic difficulty adjustment (DDA) is the established way to hold that balance** ([Zohaib 2018, *Advances in HCI*](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1155/2018/5681652)) — this is EndeavorOTC's "gets harder as you play," generalized. All three games share **one formula** (`dda.js`): every 15 seconds of play, the score earned in that window is compared against a par for your current level, and the level moves by `round(3 · log₂(scored / par))`. It's symmetric (double par → +3 levels, half par → −3), responsive (4× par jumps +6 in a single tick), and self-limiting because par climbs with level — so it converges on *your* edge within about a minute and then holds there. Level persists across sessions, so a returning player starts warm.
- **A short adaptive game can induce flow**: the validated lab paradigm is ~6 minutes of adaptive Tetris ([*Behavior Research Methods* 2023](https://link.springer.com/article/10.3758/s13428-023-02251-w)); practitioners recommend ~3 minutes of a flow-inducing activity before deep work. All three games are sized as a primer (a few minutes), not a destination.
- **A single game session raises dopamine and sharpens attention immediately after** — ventral-striatum dopamine release and improved selective attention post-play ([Frontiers Psychiatry 2025, fNIRS](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychiatry/articles/10.3389/fpsyt.2025.1640142/full); [Frontiers Human Neuroscience 2015](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/human-neuroscience/articles/10.3389/fnhum.2015.00040/full)). The handoff rides that window.

See "FLUX," "STAX," and "PULSE" above for the mechanism specific to each game and the state it targets.

### The research-backed differentiators (roadmap — not yet shipped)

**Dual-mode accountability** (from arXiv 2509.12153, and something no commercial tool currently ships):
- **Ambient mode (low-pressure):** solo, local-only, a scripted companion with event-tied captions (idle → "still here", progress → "nice, keep going", slip → "no worries, back to it"). This part is shipped — it's Alongside's companion today.
- **Peer mode (high-accountability):** live co-working via WebRTC with visible shared progress. Phase 2, not yet built.
- Design intent: user picks per session; both modes showed the same ~27% efficiency gain in the underlying study, so neither should be "lite."

**Anti-burnout gamification (SDT guardrails):**
- **No punishing streaks.** Miss a day → the world is a little overgrown, never dead. Comeback-friendly.
- **Rewards are competence feedback, not bribery** — the world reflects effort, it doesn't dangle carrots ("your garden grew," not "unlock next level in 3 sessions").
- **Autonomy:** user sets session length, companion chattiness, goals. Flexible pace.
- **Relatedness:** peer mode (and the ambient companion) meet the SDT need directly.

## What ships in Phase 1 (in this repo)

A static PWA — installable, offline-capable, zero server, genuinely free forever:

1. Focus-session timer (15 / 25 / 45 / custom, defaults to 25 per adult evidence — the same daily dose EndeavorOTC's adult trial used, [npj Mental Health Research 2024](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11187123/)).
2. Ambient companion — a calm on-screen presence with event-tied captions.
3. Session intention + reflection — one-line "what am I working on" (implementation intention) at start, focus + mood rating at end.
4. The growing world — each session plants a flower whose height/color reflects that session's focus and mood ratings.
5. Local data only — IndexedDB / localStorage; no account, no signup, exportable JSON. Privacy-first by default.

## Roadmap

- **Phase 2 — Peer rooms:** WebRTC (browser P2P, no media-server cost) + a small signaling worker on a free tier. Camera optional. Flexible / drop-in scheduling (rigid scheduling was a documented failure mode).
- **Phase 3 — CBT skills layer:** attexis-style micro-cards (60–120s), sequenced psychoeducation → attention techniques → self-image. Best adult evidence + best retention.
- **Phase 4 — Community + optional lab study:** self-host docs, themed rooms; if traction warrants it, partner with an ADHD research lab for a proper controlled study. An open, free, evidence-generating tool is a genuinely publishable contribution.

## Measurement & the "results" bar

- **Personal (Phase 1):** sessions completed, focus-minutes, per-session focus/mood ratings, optional biweekly ASRS-v1.1 self-report (free WHO scale) — shown as *your* trend, framed as personal data, not diagnosis.
- **Community (later):** retention curves (the metric that actually matters given the dropout evidence), opt-in aggregate focus-minutes. Never sold, never shared.

## Risks & honesty

- **We make no treatment claims.** Alongside is a wellness tool. If ADHD is affecting your life, please talk to a clinician.
- **The efficacy evidence for body doubling is early.** The +27–30% task-efficiency finding is from a small controlled study in VR ([arXiv 2509.12153](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.12153v1)); CHADD's dedicated study is not yet published. We're building on the strongest available real-world signal, not proven treatment effects.
- **Brain-training in general has overclaimed.** See [Simons et al., *PSPI* 2016](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/1529100616661983). Alongside deliberately isn't a brain-training game.
- **Retention is the hardest problem.** Endeavor's 34% adult dropout is our benchmark to beat. Every design choice in this document is aimed at that.
